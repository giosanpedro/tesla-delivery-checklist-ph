Walk all four sides. Check the hood, all four doors, trunk lid, and frunk lid. Pay close attention to the long door-to-quarter-panel gap at the rear. Sedans tend to show misalignment here more visibly than SUVs.
Critical
Check in multiple lighting angles. Inspect A-pillars, roof rails, door edges, sills, and the trunk lid. Lighter colors hide less than darker ones. Inspect equally regardless of color.
Critical
Inspect windshield, all door windows, the full-length glass roof, and the rear window. The front door windows are acoustic laminated glass; look for delamination bubbles along the edges. Chips may be exempt from warranty after delivery.
Confirm both sides light up evenly and that the DRL signature looks consistent left-to-right. Check for any condensation inside the housing.
The full-width rear light bar spans the entire trunk lid. Activate brake lights and tail lights and step back to check for dark spots, uneven brightness, or sections that don't illuminate. Any dead segment is a warranty item. Flag it before signing.
Test front and rear turn signals, reverse lights, and rear fog lights. Use the Controls menu on the display to cycle through all light functions.
Verify wheel size and tire spec match your order confirmation. Check all four wheels for curb rash or shipping damage. Performance variants have distinctive red brake calipers visible through the spokes. Confirm they're present and undamaged.
Walk up to each door with the key; handles should extend automatically. Push back in firmly after. Any sticking, slow travel, or failure to present should be flagged before leaving.
Front, rear, both B-pillars, and the fender-mounted repeater cameras. Verify each feed appears on the display and the image is clear with no distortion.
Open via the display and via the wall plug press. Check for paint damage around the port door and confirm the indicator light activates on connection.
The Model 3 has a smaller front trunk than the Model Y. Check that it closes flush with a firm push and the latch engages cleanly. For the trunk: it's a traditional sedan lid, not a hatchback. Check the power open/close and confirm the weather seal sits flush all around.
Press along each door seal. Gaps or lifted sections can cause wind noise and water intrusion. Any squeaks at this stage are worth noting.
Critical
Check the dash (lower left, windshield side), driver's door jamb sticker, and your registration papers. All three must match exactly.

Interior Inspection
0 / 0 items
▼
Inspect all five seats across both rows. Check seams, headrests, and bolster edges. Light-colored interiors show flaws more readily. Take your time here regardless of trim color.
Go to Climate on the display and activate seat ventilation on both driver and passenger sides. You should feel airflow within 30 seconds. A critical check while you're still at the showroom.
Click and pull each belt sharply to verify the locking mechanism. Slowly release and confirm full retraction at all five seating positions. Don't skip the rear row.
Critical
This Model 3 has a fixed full-length glass roof with no motorized interior shade. Look up from both rows for cracks or delamination (cloudy patches between glass layers). Check the perimeter seal between glass and headliner. There is nothing to slide or fold. If any panel moves, flag it.
Run your hand along the panel edges. The door cards have integrated ambient lighting strips; press gently to confirm panels are fully seated. Listen for creaks when pushing softly.
Swipe through a white background (display brightness settings) to check for dead pixels. Tap all four corners and all edges to confirm consistent touch registration. Any dead zone or mark is a warranty item. Photograph it before signing.
A dedicated 8-inch screen for rear passengers is mounted on the back of the center console. Confirm it powers on, displays content, and responds to touch across the full surface. Check for dead pixels by navigating to a solid white background.
This Model 3 has a round steering wheel, not a yoke. Check the rim surface for blemishes or uneven stitching. Confirm all physical buttons click and that both scroll wheels register input on the main display.
Ambient LED strips run along the door cards and dashboard. Go to Controls > Lights > Ambient Light and cycle through colors. Every strip should illuminate evenly with no dead sections. An unlit segment is a warranty item to document now.
Test all four windows. Check auto-up/down on the driver's window. Confirm no rattling or resistance in the window channels.
Check both rows. Confirm all-weather floor mats are present and clip in securely (if ordered). Lift any loose mats to check the carpet underneath.
The center console features a floating design with open storage underneath. Open and close the armrest. Check the wireless charging pad surface and confirm both phone slots engage when you place a device.
Test via Controls > Lights on the display. Check that courtesy lights on all door sills activate on opening. Any non-illuminating section is a warranty item.

Critical
Go to Software on the display and confirm the car identifies as the correct variant (Premium or Performance) and drivetrain (RWD or AWD). Cross-check against your purchase agreement and door jamb sticker before signing. Performance buyers should also verify Track Mode is available under Driving settings.
Open the app before leaving. Confirm you can lock/unlock, check battery state, and view the camera feed remotely. VIN shown in the app must match the car.
Tap each card to the B-pillar reader. You should receive two key cards at delivery. Set up your phone key as backup before leaving the showroom.
Press the steering wheel center pad. Quick tap only. No need to hold. Confirm the sound is clean with no distortion.
Enable from the display. Test Auto, Low, and High. Trigger the washer fluid spray. Check for streaking or skipping on either wiper blade.
Test cooling and heating. This model includes a heat pump standard. Confirm heating is efficient even at low blower settings. Check airflow from all front and rear vents. Test front and rear defrost modes.
Play audio from Bluetooth or the built-in system and pan across all channels: front, rear, and surround. Make sure every speaker position produces clear sound with no distortion or silence. A dead channel is a warranty item to flag before signing.
Pair your phone. Test call audio and media playback. Confirm microphone pickup on a test call. Make sure your phone key is also set up via the app before leaving.
Test at minimum one front port and one rear port. Confirm the dual wireless charging pad engages for both phones simultaneously.
If you can move the car in the lot, release the accelerator at low speed and confirm the regen pull feels consistent. Check that steering response feels natural and linear, with no dead spots or inconsistencies through the turn range.
Enable both in Safety settings. Walk away with your phone key and confirm the car locks and the camera icon appears on the display. Sentry Mode requires a USB drive to record footage.
Check Service > Camera Calibration. New deliveries often show "Calibration in progress"; this is normal and clears within a few drives. Note any error states and flag them to your delivery advisor.
Go to Software on the display. Note the version number. If an update is pending, schedule it before your first long drive. Ask your delivery advisor if there are any known issues with the current version.

Confirm policy number, coverage dates, and emergency contact number before driving off. Tesla PH typically bundles first-year insurance at delivery. Verify the provider and coverage scope match what was promised.
Critical
CTPL is legally required to drive in the Philippines. Verify it is active and included. The sticker should be affixed to the windshield before you drive away.
Standard Tesla delivery includes two key cards. Store one at home as a dedicated backup. Never keep both cards in the same bag as your daily carry.
Check the frunk for the mobile charging cable and wall adapter. This is your portable Level 1/2 charger for home use until a dedicated wall connector is installed.
The Model 3 does not include a spare tire. Confirm a tire inflator/repair kit is present and check its location (usually in the trunk under the floor panel). Verify the kit is not expired.
Save your delivery advisor's direct number before you leave. Any post-delivery issues in the first 72 hours are easiest to resolve through a direct contact rather than the general hotline.
Do NOT sign until every item above is checked. Signing the acceptance form means you are taking the car as-is. Note any exceptions in writing before signing. Photograph anything you flag.

This checklist is a starting point, not a substitute for your own judgment. Not every defect or issue will appear here.
